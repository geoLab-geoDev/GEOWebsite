GEO is a participatory monitoring initiative for the education sector in the Philippines. It aims to continuously improve the quality of education services by bringing the participation of citizens and communities into the governance of public schools in the country. It started in 2011 as an experimental partnership project with the Department of Education along the advocacy for access to information and social accountability.

Meet the team

Don Parafina

Project Manager, CheckMySchool (CMS)
executive.director@ansa-eap.net

Redempto S. Parafina, or Dondon, is the Executive Director of the Affiliated Network for Social Accountability in East Asia and the Pacific (ANSA-EAP) Foundation, Inc. As an international development practitioner, he has recognized track record in civil society networking, capacity building and advancing innovations in citizen participation in governance. With over fourteen years of experience, he is consulted on the technical application of social accountability in various fields and sectors, such as education, health, public procurement, ICT, youth/children, among others. His work has spanned the Philippines and various East Asian countries. He is also a Senior Associate at the Ateneo de Manila Universityâ€™s School of Government, where he has spearheaded various projects. He is a graduate student of philosophy, a boy scout and a dragonboat paddler.

Wilson Villones

Project Coordinator, CheckMySchool (CMS)
wvillones@ansa-eap.net

Wilson Villones is a researcher of the Affiliated Network for Social Accountability in East Asia and the Pacific. At present, he is the Project Coordinator of CheckMySchool. Mr. Villones has been working on a variety of governance projects, designing programs on monitoring for citizen groups, government institutions, international development partners and various other stakeholders. More recently, his works focused on citizen engagement, social accountability, transparency, M&E, and open data. Mr. Villones is also consulted on several government and CSO projects.

Darlene Motil

Communications Consultant, CheckMySchool (CMS)
dmotil@ansa-eap.net

Darlene Motil works as Communications Specialist at CMS. Since earning her degree in Communication in 2013, she had been working for non-government organizations. She writes and publishes contents on websites and the social media; she also does graphic designs. As of writing, Darlene is trying to capture, in the form of videos and photos, the impact of CheckMySchool in the grassroots. Darlene wants to put her skills to good use by working for organizations with forward-looking initiatives and genuine intention to help its beneficiaries.

Heather Baier

Map Specialist, CheckMySchool (CMS)
hmbaier@email.wm.edu

Heather Baier is a senior at the College of William and Mary majoring in Data Science with a minor in Computer Science. Her interests include developing geospatial applications and using data to better inform aid allocation. She started working with CheckMySchool in May 2018 and expects to graduate in May 2020. Heather developed the open data platform and designed the original School Neediness Index.

Matthew Crittenden

Data Specialist, CheckMySchool (CMS)
micrittenden@email.wm.edu

Matthew Crittenden is a James Monroe Scholar at the College of William & Mary double-majoring in International Relations and Data Science. His main research interests include econometric modelling of institutional accountability and aid efficacy, community-based participatory development, and harnessing data to inform policy decisions. He started working with CheckMySchool in May 2019 and expects to graduate in May 2021. Matt devleoped the CheckMySchool app analytics dashboard.
